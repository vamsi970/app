Safety Tips

At Home

  • Lock doors and windows, even during the day.
  • Install security systems like alarms or surveillance cameras.
  • Be cautious with strangers; verify identities before opening the door.
  • Avoid sharing location on social media.
  • Keep emergency contacts handy and know local emergency numbers.

In Shopping Malls

  • Stay aware of surroundings and notice exits.
  • Guard belongings and keep bags close to avoid pickpocketing.
  • Avoid isolated areas like remote restrooms or parking spots.
  • Travel light and avoid carrying too many bags.
  • Park near entrances, especially after dark.

While Traveling

  • Keep important items like passport and cash close to you.
  • Blend in by dressing to fit the local culture.
  • Share your itinerary with family or friends.
  • Use location-sharing apps to keep someone updated.
  • Avoid strangers in hotels, and don‚Äôt disclose your room number.

Online Safety

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account.
  • Be cautious of clicking on suspicious links.
  • Limit personal information shared on social media.
  • Use two-factor authentication for extra security.
  • Update privacy settings on social media platforms.

At Parties

  • Go with friends and leave together.
  • Watch your drink and never leave it unattended.
  • Be aware of exits in case of emergencies.
  • Limit alcohol consumption to stay alert.
  • Have a backup transportation plan.

Out Alone

  • Walk with confidence to avoid attracting unwanted attention.
  • Stay in well-lit areas and avoid shortcuts.
  • Avoid using headphones; stay aware of surroundings.
  • Carry a personal safety device, like pepper spray or a whistle.
  • If followed, head to a public place or group.

In the Car

  • Lock doors immediately after entering or exiting.
  • Park in well-lit areas and check around your car before entering.
  • Keep valuables out of sight to avoid break-ins.
  • If you suspect you‚Äôre being followed, drive to a public place or police station.
  • Check the backseat before entering the car.

At Work

  • Avoid staying late alone; leave with coworkers if possible.
  • Know emergency exits and security contact information.
  • Use lockers or locked drawers for personal belongings.
  • Report any suspicious behavior to HR or security.
  • Maintain professional boundaries with colleagues.

In Schools and Colleges

  • Know the campus security contacts.
  • Use campus transport services at night, like shuttles or escorts.
  • Exercise caution with new friends and relationships.
  • Always keep dorm rooms locked, even for short absences.
  • Stick to populated study areas, especially at night.

Public Transport

  • Sit near the driver or conductor when possible.
  • Avoid empty compartments, especially at night.
  • Stay alert and keep belongings close.
  • Don‚Äôt share travel details with strangers.
  • Know the route to avoid looking lost or confused.

Exercising Outdoors

  • Exercise during daylight or in well-lit areas.
  • Use a running app with emergency alert features.
  • Inform someone of your route and expected return time.
  • Avoid using headphones or keep one earbud out to stay alert.
  • Vary your routes and times to reduce predictability.
